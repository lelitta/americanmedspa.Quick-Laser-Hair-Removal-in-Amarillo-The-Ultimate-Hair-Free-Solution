How Laser Hair Removal Works

Laser hair removal works by emitting a concentrated beam of light that targets the pigment in the hair. This light is absorbed by the melanin, which heats and damages the hair follicle. The damaged follicle is then unable to grow new hair.

Because hair grows in different cycles, multiple sessions are needed to target hair in various growth stages. The treatment is typically spaced several weeks apart to allow the hair follicles to move through their growth cycle, resulting in the most effective hair removal.
